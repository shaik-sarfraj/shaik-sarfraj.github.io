## Overview

This research develops a GIS-based composite spatial index to evaluate variations in quality of public life across urban precincts in Sydney.

The study investigates how environmental, social, accessibility, and urban form characteristics influence public life outcomes by integrating diverse spatial datasets into a measurable analytical framework.

The research combines Geographic Information Systems (GIS), spatial modelling, statistical analysis, and Python-supported geospatial workflows to transform complex urban datasets into spatial decision-support outputs.

The framework links statistical evaluation at the SA2 administrative level with continuous raster-based spatial modelling, enabling both precinct-level comparison and fine-scale understanding of spatial variations.

## Research Framework

The study evaluates quality of public life through multiple dimensions including:

- Environmental conditions, including green space availability, tree canopy coverage, and non-built surface availability.
- Social characteristics, including volunteering rates and homelessness indicators.
- Accessibility factors, including transport connectivity and healthcare accessibility.
- Urban structure indicators, including land-use diversity and heritage areas.

The framework integrates these indicators to understand how spatial conditions collectively influence public life performance across urban areas.


## Methodology


<img src="/assets/images/projects/thesis/thesis-methodology.jpeg" alt="Research Methodology" class="project-image">


The workflow integrates spatial data processing, statistical computation, and GIS-based modelling through the following stages:


<img src="/assets/images/projects/thesis/thesis-dataprocessing.jpeg" alt="Spatial Data Processing and Standardisation" class="project-image">


- Collection and preparation of spatial datasets from sources including Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS), NSW Open Data, SEED, OpenStreetMap, and Overture Maps.
- Processing and standardisation of multiple indicators into comparable spatial layers.
- Development of indicator-specific scoring frameworks for measuring spatial performance.
- Application of entropy-based weighting to determine indicator contribution based on spatial variation.
- Python-supported raster processing workflows to automate spatial transformation and weighted layer integration.
- Generation of a continuous quality of public life raster surface to identify spatial variations within and between urban precincts.


<img src="/assets/images/projects/thesis/thesis-raster.jpeg" alt="Composite Spatial Index Raster Output" class="project-image">


## Key Outcomes

The research outcomes include:

- Development of a GIS-based composite index integrating 11 urban indicators for evaluating quality of public life.
- Application of entropy weighting to objectively determine indicator importance.
- Integration of SA2-level statistical analysis with raster-based spatial modelling for multi-scale evaluation.
- Development of Python-assisted geospatial workflows for spatial data processing and analysis.
- Creation of spatial decision-support outputs capable of identifying variations in public space performance across urban precincts.


## Research Contribution

The study demonstrates how GIS and computational methods can support evidence-based urban planning by converting complex spatial datasets into interpretable analytical frameworks.

The research highlights the potential of combining spatial analytics, public space evaluation, and data-driven modelling to better understand the relationship between urban environments and quality of public life.
