## Overview

Developed as part of PLAN7140 Land & Environment Law at UNSW Sydney, this project examines the role of statutory frameworks in guiding planning decision-making within New South Wales.

The analysis investigates how decision-makers evaluate competing considerations under Section 4.15 of the NSW Environmental Planning and Assessment Act 1979, including environmental impacts, strategic planning objectives, community interests, and broader public outcomes.

The project demonstrates the importance of legislative knowledge and policy interpretation in supporting transparent, evidence-based planning decisions.

## Legislative Analysis Framework

The analysis investigates:

- The role of Section 4.15 considerations in guiding development assessment decisions.
- The interaction between environmental impacts, strategic planning objectives, and community expectations.
- How legislative priorities influence the balancing of competing planning outcomes.
- The importance of statutory frameworks in supporting consistent and transparent planning practice.

## Professional Relevance

This project strengthened my understanding of the legislative foundations underpinning urban planning practice in NSW, particularly the role of statutory assessment frameworks in shaping development outcomes.

It demonstrates the integration of planning knowledge, policy interpretation, and legal frameworks required for effective planning practice within the Australian planning system.
