## Overview

An urban design and transit-oriented development framework developed for the Belmore precinct as part of my UNSW Master of City Planning coursework.

The project explores opportunities to accommodate future growth while enhancing the quality of the public realm through improved built form outcomes, pedestrian connectivity, open space integration, and place-based design strategies.

The framework investigates the relationship between transport accessibility, urban form, and community needs to develop design interventions that support a more connected, liveable, and people-focused precinct.

![Belmore Design Proposal](/assets/images/projects/belmore/belmore-01.png)

## Design Approach

The project applies an urban design methodology focused on:

- Assessment of existing precinct conditions, including land use, built form, movement networks, and public space characteristics.
- Identification of opportunities for improved connectivity, pedestrian experience, and public realm quality.
- Development of built form strategies responding to transport accessibility and future population growth.
- Integration of urban design principles including active frontages, mixed-use development, public space enhancement, and human-scale environments.

![Belmore Design Strategy](/assets/images/projects/belmore/belmore-02.png)

## Key Design Outcomes

The proposed framework focuses on:

- Improving pedestrian connectivity and movement within the precinct.
- Enhancing public spaces through better integration of landscape, activity, and community functions.
- Supporting transit-oriented growth through context-sensitive built form strategies.
- Creating a stronger relationship between development, transport infrastructure, and public life.
