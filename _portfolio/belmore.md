## Overview

Developed as part of the UNSW Master of City Planning program, this project proposes a transit-oriented development framework for the Belmore precinct.

The project investigates how increased residential growth can be supported through improved built form, pedestrian connectivity, public space networks, and place-based urban design interventions.

The framework integrates geospatial analysis, urban design principles, and planning considerations to develop strategies that enhance accessibility, liveability, and public realm quality.

## Site Context

Belmore is an established urban centre in Sydney's south-west, characterised by strong rail accessibility, diverse land uses, and opportunities for transit-oriented intensification.

The project examined existing conditions including:

- Transport accessibility
- Existing built form patterns
- Public space availability
- Pedestrian movement networks
- Opportunities for future growth

![Belmore Design Proposal](/assets/images/projects/belmore/belmore-01.png)

## Methodology & Design Framework

The project applies an urban design methodology focused on:

- Assessment of existing precinct conditions, including land use, built form, movement networks, and public space characteristics.
- Identification of opportunities for improved connectivity, pedestrian experience, and public realm quality.
- Development of built form strategies responding to transport accessibility and future population growth.
- Integration of urban design principles including active frontages, mixed-use development, public space enhancement, and human-scale environments.

![Belmore Design Strategy](/assets/images/projects/belmore/belmore-02.png)

## Key Design Outcomes

The proposed framework identifies several strategies:

### Improved Pedestrian Network

Strengthening pedestrian movement through better connections between transport infrastructure, public spaces, and surrounding land uses.

### Enhanced Public Realm

Creating opportunities for improved open spaces, landscape integration, and community-focused activity areas.

### Transit-Oriented Growth

Supporting increased development intensity through context-sensitive built form strategies around existing transport accessibility.

### Place-Based Urban Design

Balancing future growth with local character through active frontages, human-scale environments, and improved street experiences.
