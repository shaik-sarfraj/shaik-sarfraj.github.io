## Overview

An undergraduate thesis investigating the impact of increasing urban activities on mobility performance within inner-city areas and developing a regulatory framework for improving transport system efficiency.

The research addresses the challenge of managing mobility in mature urban areas where existing road infrastructure faces increasing pressure from land-use intensification, population growth, and concentrated activity patterns. The study examines how different urban functions influence road-level performance and explores strategies to align land-use development with transport capacity.

The research develops an integrated spatial assessment framework combining Geographic Information Systems (GIS), multi-criteria decision analysis, traffic performance evaluation, and urban activity mapping to identify areas experiencing high carrying pressure and limited infrastructure capacity.

The framework aims to support evidence-based decision-making by prioritising road stretches for targeted interventions and demonstrating how spatial analytics can inform sustainable urban mobility planning.

<img src="/assets/images/projects/gis/ugthesis-1.png" alt="Undergraduate Thesis">

## Analytical Framework

The study applies a GIS-based spatial modelling approach through the following stages:

- Identification of urban activity generators and spatial factors influencing mobility demand, including land use intensity, population density, employment centres, transport nodes, tourist destinations, and development patterns.
- Development of an Urban Activity Concentration Map using spatial indicators to identify areas generating higher levels of movement and travel demand.
- Assessment of road carrying pressure by analysing variables including building footprint intensity, trip attraction points, land value, transport infrastructure changes, and changes in developable land.
- Evaluation of road carrying capacity through traffic performance indicators including level of service, traffic flow characteristics, speed-density relationships, and environmental impacts.
- Application of Fuzzy Analytical Hierarchy Process (FAHP) weighting to combine multiple spatial parameters and generate composite carrying pressure and carrying capacity indices.
- Statistical analysis using spatial relationships and correlation techniques to evaluate the influence of surrounding land-use activities on road-level travel speeds.
- Development of priority intervention zones by integrating carrying pressure, carrying capacity, and carrying potential assessments.

<img src="/assets/images/projects/gis/ugthesis-2.png" alt="Undergraduate Thesis">

## Key Outcomes

The research generated spatial decision-support outputs including:

- Urban activity concentration maps identifying areas experiencing intensified mobility demand.
- Ward-level carrying pressure assessments to evaluate infrastructure stress.
- Road-level carrying capacity analysis for selected inner-city corridors.
- Composite indices for prioritising mobility improvement strategies.
- Spatial, regulatory, and physical intervention frameworks for improving urban mobility performance.

The study demonstrates the role of GIS-based analytical frameworks in bridging land-use planning and transportation management, providing a replicable approach for assessing mobility challenges within rapidly growing urban areas.
